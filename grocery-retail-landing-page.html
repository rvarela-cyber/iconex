Why Linerless Labels Are the Smart Choice for Modern Grocery Operations

Retailers and grocers must update prices frequently, manage promotions in real time, and fulfill online orders across channels. Labels carry pricing, barcodes, dates, and routing details that must be accurate to protect margin and customer trust.

Linerless labels are pressure‑sensitive labels supplied without a release liner. They enable on‑demand printing and direct application, with no peel‑and‑discard step. This simple change supports faster labeling, higher roll capacity, and much less waste.

Labor and Productivity Gains in Store

In high‑volume grocery and retail formats, associates may apply hundreds or thousands of labels daily. Traditional linered labels require peeling each label, frequent roll changes, and managing liner scrap. This adds friction at busy times.

Transform Grocery Operations with Linerless Labels

Linerless technology can provide roughly 40–60% more labels per roll of equivalent diameter than linered (traditional) labels, meaning fewer interruptions for roll changes and more continuous uptime at scale or at the printer.

  • Faster application: With no liner to remove, labels can be printed and applied immediately, speeding price changes, markdowns, and weigh‑scale operations at service counters.
  • Less downtime and cleanup: Stores avoid liner trash on floors and work surfaces, lowering slip risks and reducing time spent handling waste instead of serving customers.

A busy fresh department that reduces roll changes and per‑label handling can free up valuable time. Associates can spend more minutes daily on stocking, guest service, and staging, not label management.

Accuracy to Protect Store Margins and Experience

Pricing or labeling errors erode margins through incorrect scans, rework, and potential compliance issues, while fulfillment mistakes damage trust and increase service costs. Consistent, accurate labeling underpins inventory visibility, correct shelf prices, and error‑free online orders.

How Linerless Labels Support Accuracy Throughout the Store

  • Clear, on‑demand data: Linerless printers produce crisp barcodes, dates, and product information on demand, reducing misalignment and print issues that can occur with traditional labels.
  • Better inventory tracking: Including barcodes and QR codes on linerless labels improves scan rates and inventory accuracy, helping avoid stockouts, overstocking, and mis‑picks.
  • Fewer handling errors: Eliminating liner peel steps reduces opportunities for labels to stick to the wrong surface or get damaged before application.

Direct links exist between high order accuracy and customer loyalty. Shoppers will return and recommend stores that get orders right. Accurate labeling strengthens this, especially in omnichannel fulfillment where each item must match the order.

Customer Lifetime Value and Omnichannel Loyalty

Order and price accuracy drive satisfaction, reviews, and repeat retail or grocery business. When customers get correct items at the expected price—on shelf, curbside, or home delivery—they trust the brand and are more likely to return and recommend the store.

Research suggests customers abandon brands after inaccurate orders. Satisfied customers are more likely to buy again and refer others. Fewer mislabels and scan errors help linerless programs protect the shopping experience and boost customer lifetime value.

Use Cases Across Retail and Grocery Operations

Linerless labels can be deployed across multiple workflows in a single store or network, using common media where appropriate to simplify purchasing and training.

Fresh and Weigh‑Scale Labeling

Labels with variable lengths and printed with prices per weight, used for items like meat, seafood, deli, bakery, and produce. High‑capacity rolls help keep service counters operating smoothly.

Price Changes and Markdowns

Fast, on‑demand shelf labels and markdown stickers that support dynamic pricing and promotion execution without piles of liner waste.

Click‑and‑Collect and Ship‑from‑Store

Pick labels and tote labels that carry item, order, and routing detail for accurate picking and staging in omnichannel fulfillment.

Inventory and Back‑Room Labeling

Labels with barcodes, often attached to totes, pallets, and stockroom locations, to improve inventory visibility and replenishment accuracy.
