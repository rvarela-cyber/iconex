Why Smart E‑Commerce Businesses Are Making the Switch to Linerless Labels

E‑commerce and omnichannel brands live and die by fulfillment speed, accuracy, and cost‑to‑serve. Every extra step in labeling, every roll change, and every mis‑picked or mis‑labeled carton translates into higher labor cost, more returns, and lower customer lifetime value.

Linerless labels remove the backing liner entirely, enabling continuous rolls with variable label lengths that can be cut on demand. This delivers more labels per roll, faster application, less waste, and simpler inventory of label SKUs across picking, packing, and shipping stations.

Picking & Selection Labels

Accurate, efficient picking is the foundation of e‑commerce order accuracy. When pickers can't clearly see what to pull, where it goes, or which order it belongs to, errors multiply, and the cost of corrections rises quickly.

Game‑Changing Improvement in Picking and Selection

  • More labels per roll at pick stations: With up to 40–50% more labels per roll, pick carts, handheld, or wearable printers need fewer roll changes during peak waves, keeping pickers on the floor and in flow.
  • Clear item and order identifiers: On‑demand printed pick labels can carry SKU, description, quantity, and order or tote IDs, making it easier to match items to the correct container the first time.
  • Faster, cleaner application: Eliminating liners removes peel‑and‑discard motions and liner scraps in aisles, supporting both productivity and safety in busy warehouses.

Better picking accuracy directly contributes to higher order accuracy rates, which in turn reduce returns, refunds, and negative reviews that erode margins and customer loyalty.

Pack & Ship Operations

The pack station is where item‑level accuracy, branding, and shipping details come together. Labels for cartons, bags, and inserts must be correct and legible; otherwise, shipments are misrouted, parcels are returned, and customer service workloads spike.

Strengthening Pack and Ship Workflows

  • Fewer roll changes at pack benches: High‑capacity linerless rolls reduce interruptions for label reloads, which is especially valuable when packers are handling a continuous stream of cartons.
  • Flexible label lengths for different parcels: Variable‑length labels allow one roll to cover small parcels, poly mailers, and larger cartons, simplifying label inventory and minimizing changeovers.
  • Reduced material and freight costs: More labels per roll and no liners mean fewer rolls to purchase, store, and ship into fulfillment centers, trimming operational costs.

E‑commerce service level agreements increasingly rely on high order accuracy and on‑time shipment metrics, and efficient, error‑resistant labeling at pack and ship is central to meeting these targets.

Last‑Mile Tracking & Visibility

From the moment an order is printed in the warehouse to final delivery, tracking data drives customer confidence and internal efficiency. Robust labeling with clear barcodes and identifiers is essential for scan‑based tracking, exception handling, and analytics across the fulfillment network.

End‑To‑End Tracking with Linerless Labels

  • Better scan performance: High‑quality barcodes and QR codes printed on linerless labels improve scan rates at induction, sorting, loading, and last‑mile handoff.
  • Consistent identifiers across workflows: Using consistent linerless label formats for totes, pallets, and cartons support unified tracking without juggling multiple label SKUs.
  • Data for continuous improvement: A reliable scan events feed provides more accurate performance metrics (order accuracy, on‑time rate, dwell time), enabling targeted process improvements to further enhance customer experience.

High order accuracy rates—approaching 98–99.9%—are strongly correlated with higher customer satisfaction, repeat purchases, and positive word‑of‑mouth, all of which lift customer lifetime value.
